## Portal
<PageDescription>
Welcome to the IMiC Data Portal Tutorial Page, a resource for exploring curated data on human milk composition from the International Milk Composition (IMiC) Consortium and its global study partners. This user-friendly portal allows researchers, healthcare professionals, and policymakers to access harmonized data organized into studies, datasets, and data variables. Gain insights into the nutritional and non-nutritional components of human milk, examine correlations, and explore the impact of various factors on milk composition. Join us in advancing maternal, newborn, and infant nutrition recommendations through evidence-based decision-making on the IMiC Data Portal. This site's purpose is to provide users with a guide on how to use the portal.

</PageDescription>

## IMiC
<PageDescription>
The International Milk Composition (IMiC) Consortium was established in 2020 to analyze and understand the composition of human milk. It aims to identify the variations in milk components between women and different settings, and how these components are correlated with each other. The consortium will collect milk samples from 1200 mother-infant pairs across four diverse locations and analyze them for various nutrients, growth factors, immunoglobulins, and microbes. The data will be stored in a central database, and statistical methods will be used for analysis. The ultimate goal is to inform recommendations and interventions for maternal, newborn, and infant nutrition based on the findings. The IMiC Consortium is co-directed by Dr. Meghan Azad and Natalie Rodriguez, and it is funded by a $5,000,000 investment from the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation Maternal, Newborn & Child Health Strategy. The Manitoba Interdisciplinary Lactation Centre (MILC) Biorepository serves as the central storage location for the milk samples.
</PageDescription>
